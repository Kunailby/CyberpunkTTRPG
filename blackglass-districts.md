# Blackglass

Blackglass is a vertical cyberpunk city-state built from flood barriers, black glass towers, rusted transit rings, and corporate debt. Its districts are dense, surveilled, and half-owned by factions that prefer contracts over laws.

## Districts

### Sleeper Stack
Coffin apartments welded into a leaning tower of rain, debt, and stolen power. Most residents live six locks deep and know better than to ask who pays the hallway cameras.

### Null Market
A lightless trade arcade where contraband is priced in credits, secrets, or blood. If it can be stolen, cloned, cracked, or implanted, someone here has it under the counter.

### Glasshook Wharf
Flooded cargo docks under hologram fog, where smugglers work by sonar and muzzle flash. The old cranes still move at night, even when nobody admits sending the command.

### Ferrum Yards
Autofoundries, rail spines, and union ghosts packed into a district of heat and metal fatigue. The machines never clock out, and neither do the crews trapped in their contracts.

### Mercy Nine
Clinic blocks and chop-shops stacked wall to wall. You leave fixed, owned, or missing parts, depending on who paid the surgeon first.

### Crown Relay
Corporate broadcast towers above the smog, polished enough to look clean from a distance. Every window is a camera with better manners.

### Static Gardens
A dead oxygen park threaded with pirate antennas, old memorials, and wet neon. People come here to vanish into signal noise or leave offerings for names the city erased.

### Redline Arcade
Gambling dens, combat sims, and joywire parlors tuned to ruin people efficiently. Every pleasure has a meter, and every meter runs faster than expected.

### Mirevault
Submerged server catacombs cooled by black water. Data divers go down rich or do not come up at all.

### Kilo Shrine
Data temples and signal monks selling encrypted absolution beside illegal uplinks. Confession here is just another kind of upload.

### Threadneedle
Identity tailors, face printers, and memory counterfeiters behind paper-thin storefronts. A clean name costs extra; a believable past costs more.

### The Ash Loop
A circular transit slum under the maglev line, loud enough to hide almost anything. Deals happen between train screams, and bodies are counted by service delays.
